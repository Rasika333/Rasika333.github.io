J. D. Salinger (1919 — 2010)

Jerome David Salinger was an American writer best known for his 1951 novel The Catcher in the Rye.Before its publication, Salinger published several short stories in Story magazine and served in World War II. Among other accomplishments, he takes part in the Battle of the Bulge and, later, enters Kaufering, a sub-camp of Dachau. He also forges a strong friendship with war correspondent Ernest Hemingway. Salinger continues his writing career during the war, carting his typewriter around in his Jeep. In 1948, his critically acclaimed story A Perfect Day for Bananafish appeared in The New Yorker, which published much of his later work.

Regarding "The Catcher in the Rye", Salinger's depiction of adolescent alienation and loss of innocence in the protagonist Holden Caulfield was influential, especially among adolescent readers.The novel was widely read and controversial,and its success led to public attention and scrutiny.The Catcher in the Rye has a legacy of the most censored as well as the most taught book in schools with sales reaching over 60 million. The novel also deals with complex issues of innocence, identity, belonging, loss, connection, sex, and depression. Salinger received (and rejected) numerous offers to adapt The Catcher in the Rye for the screen, including one from Samuel Goldwyn.His ex-girlfriend commented that Salinger himself would be fit to play Holden Caulfield. The infamy towards the novel became larger as it was revealed that the man who shot John Lennon read Salinger's book and was obsessed with Caulfield.

After his successful novel, he wrote short story collections — Nine Stories (1953) Franny and Zooey (1961) as well as Raise High the Roof Beam, Carpenters and Seymour: An Introduction. The last novella he published was Hapworth 16, 1924 which appeared in The New Yorker on June 19 1965.Later on in life, he lived a life of privacy and enjoyed collecting movies from the 1940s. He raised two children — Margaret and Matt Salinger

Salinger died of natural causes at his home in New Hampshire on January 27, 2010 . What he left behind was a novel that enraged as well has captivated the population with a polarizing character whom many people relate to.
